Excerpt: ADA Website Compliance Kit

Example of how your generated outline and content might be structured: 

1. Accessibility Commitment Statement
   - Short, plain-language statement about your organization’s commitment to
     making its website and digital services accessible to people with disabilities.
   - Includes a dated “last updated” line for your policy.

2. Scope of this Policy
   - Explains which websites, apps, and digital properties are covered.
   - Notes any third-party platforms where you have limited control.

3. Accessibility Standards & Approach
   - References common accessibility frameworks (for example, WCAG) as guidance
     without guaranteeing legal compliance.
   - Describes your general process for testing and improving accessibility over time.

4. Reporting Accessibility Issues
   - Provides a clear way for visitors to contact you (email, phone, or form).
   - Encourages users to include details of the issue and assistive technology in use.

FAQ

Is this legal advice or guaranteed to be compliant?

No. Comply-Desk does not provide legal advice and cannot guarantee compliance with any law or regulation. Our kits are informational starting points to help you structure your own documentation. Always review with your internal team and external counsel if needed.

What exactly do I receive after purchase?

You will receive: (1) a structured outline on the website and (2) an editable Word document (.docx) that you can download. The document includes placeholders and prompts where you can add your organization’s details, policies, and procedures.

Can I reuse the templates for multiple locations or clients?

Kits are intended for use within your own organization. If you are a consultant or agency creating materials for multiple clients, you are responsible for ensuring each output is appropriate and reviewed independently.

How often are the kits updated?

Comply-Desk may update prompts, structure, or example language over time, but we do not commit to tracking all legal or regulatory changes. You remain responsible for ensuring your documentation stays current.

Who should not use Comply-Desk?

Organizations that require formal legal opinions, highly specialized regulatory frameworks, or jurisdiction-specific approvals should work directly with qualified counsel instead of relying on templates or AI.

How can I get support?

For questions about your purchase or technical issues generating a kit, email enquiries@comply-desk.com. We cannot answer legal questions or interpret regulations.
